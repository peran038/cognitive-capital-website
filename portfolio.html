Our Portfolio

Foundational Hardware

NVIDIA

The undisputed leader in accelerated computing, providing the foundational hardware that powers the AI revolution.

Thesis

NVIDIA's full-stack approach, from silicon to software (CUDA), has created a deep, defensible moat. Their GPUs are the default choice for training and deploying large-scale AI models, giving them immense pricing power and a direct line of sight into the future of AI development.

Key Metrics

  • Data Center Revenue Growth
  • CUDA Adoption and Developer Ecosystem Size
  • Gross Margins on High-End GPUs

Risks

Intensifying competition from other chip designers and large cloud providers developing their own custom silicon. Geopolitical risks impacting the supply chain.

Cerebras Systems

Building wafer-scale processors to dramatically accelerate AI research and large model training.

Thesis

By moving beyond the limitations of traditional chip packaging, Cerebras offers a step-change in performance for specific AI workloads. Their systems are designed for researchers and enterprises pushing the boundaries of what's possible with AI, creating a high-end, specialized market segment.

Key Metrics

  • Customer Adoption in HPC and Research
  • Performance Benchmarks vs. GPU Clusters
  • Manufacturing Yield and Cost of Wafer-Scale Engines

Risks

High cost of their systems limits market size. Competition from increasingly powerful and flexible GPU clusters. The need for specialized software to leverage their unique architecture.

Groq

Developing a new category of processor, the Language Processing Unit (LPU), for ultra-low latency AI inference.

Thesis

As AI models become more integrated into real-time applications, inference speed is becoming a critical bottleneck. Groq's LPU architecture is designed from the ground up for deterministic, high-speed performance, opening up new possibilities for conversational AI, autonomous systems, and other latency-sensitive applications.

Key Metrics

  • Inference Latency Benchmarks (Tokens/Second/User)
  • Adoption by Real-Time Application Developers
  • Power Efficiency (Performance per Watt)

Risks

Competition from NVIDIA's inference-optimized GPUs. The need to build a software ecosystem around their new processor type. Market education to convince developers of the need for specialized inference hardware.

Data & AI Platforms

Databricks

The Data and AI company, providing a unified platform for data engineering, data science, and machine learning.

Thesis

Databricks is building the modern data stack around the "lakehouse" paradigm, combining the scalability of data lakes with the performance of data warehouses. This unified approach breaks down data silos and allows organizations to build and deploy AI applications on a single, coherent platform, creating strong network effects and high switching costs.

Key Metrics

  • Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)
  • Customer Growth and Net Revenue Retention
  • Adoption of their Machine Learning and AI products

Risks

Intense competition from cloud data platforms like Snowflake and the native offerings of AWS, Azure, and GCP. The complexity of the data landscape can lead to a long sales cycle.

Scale AI

The data platform for AI, providing high-quality training data for leading machine learning teams.

Thesis

High-quality, well-labeled data is the lifeblood of modern AI. Scale AI has built a technology-driven platform for data annotation and curation that is critical for developing high-performing models. As AI systems become more sophisticated, the demand for reliable, high-quality data will only increase, positioning Scale at the center of the AI development lifecycle.

Key Metrics

  • Revenue Growth from Enterprise Customers
  • Expansion into New Data Modalities (e.g., video, 3D)
  • Customer Retention in Key Verticals (e.g., autonomous vehicles)

Risks

The rise of synthetic data generation could reduce the need for human-in-the-loop labeling. Competition from other data labeling services and in-house data teams. Maintaining quality at scale is a constant operational challenge.

Hugging Face

The leading open-source platform and community for building, training, and deploying state-of-the-art machine learning models.

Thesis

Hugging Face has become the "GitHub for machine learning," creating a powerful community-driven moat. By providing the tools and infrastructure for open-source AI collaboration, they have become the central hub for AI developers, giving them a unique position to monetize through enterprise-grade services and support.

Key Metrics

  • Community Growth (Users, Models, Datasets)
  • Enterprise Customer Adoption
  • API Usage and Platform Engagement

Risks

Monetizing an open-source community is challenging. Competition from large cloud providers offering their own model repositories and MLOps platforms. The risk of the community forking key projects.

Vertical AI Applications

Tempus

A technology company advancing precision medicine through the practical application of artificial intelligence in healthcare.

Thesis

Tempus is building one of the world's largest libraries of clinical and molecular data, along with an operating system to make that data accessible and useful. By integrating this data with AI, they are creating a powerful platform for drug discovery, clinical trial optimization, and personalized patient care, creating a deep data moat in the healthcare industry.

Key Metrics

  • Size and Diversity of Proprietary Dataset
  • Partnerships with Pharmaceutical Companies and Hospitals
  • Revenue from Data Licensing and Clinical Trial Services

Risks

Navigating the complex regulatory landscape of healthcare data (e.g., HIPAA). High cost of data acquisition and sequencing. Long sales cycles with large healthcare organizations.

Anduril

A defense technology company building the future of military capabilities with AI and robotics.

Thesis

Anduril is bringing a Silicon Valley software-first mindset to the traditionally slow-moving defense industry. By developing an AI-powered operating system for defense hardware, they are creating a platform that can integrate with both their own and third-party systems, positioning them to become the command and control layer for modern warfare.

Key Metrics

  • Value and Number of Government Contracts
  • Deployment Speed of New Capabilities
  • Adoption of their Lattice OS platform

Risks

The long and unpredictable nature of government procurement cycles. Competition from established defense prime contractors. Ethical and public perception challenges associated with autonomous defense systems.

Adept

Building a new kind of AI model that can perform actions on a computer, acting as a universal collaborator for knowledge workers.

Thesis

Adept is moving beyond language models to create "action models." By training an AI to use existing software tools (e.g., Salesforce, Photoshop), they are building a natural language interface to all of enterprise software. This has the potential to fundamentally change how we interact with computers, unlocking massive productivity gains.

Key Metrics

  • Range and Complexity of Supported Software Actions
  • Reliability and Accuracy of the Action Model
  • Early Enterprise Pilot Program Success

Risks

The technical challenge of building a general-purpose action model is immense. The model's performance will be highly dependent on the user interfaces of the software it interacts with. Competition from large tech companies building similar capabilities into their own operating systems.

AI Infrastructure & DevTools

Replicate

A platform for running and sharing open-source machine learning models in the cloud.

Thesis

Replicate is abstracting away the complexity of deploying and scaling machine learning models. By providing a simple, developer-friendly API for running open-source models, they are making AI accessible to a much broader audience of developers, positioning themselves as the "Heroku for machine learning."

Key Metrics

  • Developer Adoption and API Call Volume
  • Number of Models Available on the Platform
  • Revenue Growth from Paid Plans

Risks

Competition from other model deployment platforms and the native MLOps offerings of large cloud providers. The need to keep up with the rapid pace of new model releases in the open-source community.

Anyscale

The company behind the open-source Ray project, providing a platform for building and scaling distributed applications.

Thesis

As AI workloads become larger and more complex, the need for a robust and scalable distributed computing framework is critical. Ray is becoming the standard for distributed AI, and Anyscale is building the enterprise-grade platform on top of it, allowing companies to scale their AI applications without having to become experts in distributed systems.

Key Metrics

  • Adoption of the open-source Ray project
  • Growth of Anyscale's managed platform revenue
  • Number of enterprise customers building on Ray

Risks

Competition from other distributed computing frameworks like Spark. The complexity of distributed systems can make adoption challenging for some organizations. The need to balance the open-source community with commercial interests.

Weights & Biases

The developer-first MLOps platform for tracking experiments, visualizing results, and collaborating on machine learning projects.

Thesis

Machine learning development is an iterative, experimental process. Weights & Biases provides the essential tools for managing this complexity, acting as the "system of record" for ML teams. Their developer-first focus has led to strong bottom-up adoption, creating a powerful flywheel for growth.

Key Metrics

  • Active User Growth and Engagement
  • Number of Experiments Tracked on the Platform
  • Conversion from free to paid enterprise plans

Risks

Competition from other MLOps platforms and open-source tools like MLflow. The risk of being seen as a "nice-to-have" rather than a "must-have" tool in some organizations. The need to continuously innovate to keep up with the evolving needs of ML developers.
